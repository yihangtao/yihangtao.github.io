About Me

I received my B.S. degree from the School of Information Science and Engineering, Southeast University, Nanjing, China, in 2021, and my M.S. degree from the School of Computer Science, Shanghai Jiao Tong University, Shanghai, China, in 2024. Currently, I am pursuing my PhD degree in the Department of Computer Science at City University of Hong Kong (Advisor: Prof. Yuguang "Michael" Fang). My research broadly focuses on trustworthy Spatial Intelligence for Agentic AI, particularly in Autonomous Driving.

I am actively seeking collaborators of like-minded interests. If you are interested in my work, please feel free to contact me via email: yihang.tommy@my.cityu.edu.hk.

üöÄ Research Thrust

"Envisioning a future where autonomous agents possess human-like Spatial Intelligence, seamlessly navigating and interacting with the physical world. My goal is to build trustworthy and embodied AI systems that not only perceive their environment but also understand and reason about it, bridging the gap between digital simulation and physical reality."

To achieve this vision, my research enhances the reasoning and decision-making of embodied agents, especially Autonomous Vehicles, in complex 3D environments. Aiming for a holistic Spatial Understanding, this work transcends individual sensing limits from two complementary angles: Collaboration for Extended 3D Understanding actively acquires external information to broaden perceptual boundaries, while Generation for Predictive 3D Understanding exploits internal data to predict future horizons. Both approaches converge to equip agents with a more profound and comprehensive cognition of the physical world.

Collaboration for Extended 3D Understanding

This thrust focuses on overcoming physical occlusions and sensing limits through collaborative perception. It addresses the design of efficient collaboration mechanisms for practical, degraded environments, tackling challenges like bandwidth-constrained communication and adversarial attacks. This ensures agents maintain robust perception and 3D understanding even under adverse conditions.

Generation for Predictive 3D Understanding

This thrust focuses on internalizing the physical world to empower agents to envision future horizons. By utilizing Generative Models for controllable driving scene generation and Feedforward 3DGS to recover geometric details with only unposed images, these techniques enable agents to predict future scenarios, enhancing their spatial understanding and decision-making.
