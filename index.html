Biography

Yihang Tao received the B.S. degree from the School of Information Science and Engineering, Southeast University, Nanjing, China, in 2021 and received his M.S. degree from the School of Electronic Information and Electrical Engineering, Shanghai Jiao Tong University, Shanghai, China, in 2024. Currently, he is pursuing his PhD degree in the Department of Computer Science at City University of Hong Kong (Advisor: Prof. Yuguang "Michael" Fang). His current research interests include collaborative perception, autonomous driving, and AI security. He serves as the program committee member for ICML 2025, ICLR 2025, AAAI 2025, and the reviewer for IEEE TITS, EAAI, Pattern Recognition, etc.

Research Interests

Multi-Agent Autonomous Driving

  • Multi-Agent Collaborative Perception (CP): Overcoming limitations of single-agent perception systems like occlusion and sensing range by leveraging information from multiple vehicles.
  • Multi-Agent Autonomous Driving Security: Designing defense methods crucial for secure CP as it expands the system's attack surface by leveraging information from other vehicles.

Foundation Models

  • LLM Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT) and Task Adaptation: Adapting LLMs to specific tasks, such as autonomous driving and collaborative decision-making, better and more efficiently.
  • LLM based Multi-Agent Systems: Leveraging LLM/VLM as powerful tools to build multi-agent collaborative decision-making systems for next-generation autonomous driving.
