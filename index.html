Biography

Yihang Tao received the B.S. degree from the School of Information Science and Engineering, Southeast University, Nanjing, China, in 2021 and received his M.S. degree from the School of Electronic Information and Electrical Engineering, Shanghai Jiao Tong University, Shanghai, China, in 2024. Currently, he is pursuing his PhD degree in the Department of Computer Science at City University of Hong Kong (Advisor: Prof. Yuguang "Michael" Fang). His current research interests include collaborative perception, autonomous driving, and AI security. He serves as the program committee member for ICML 2025, ICLR 2025, AAAI 2025, and the reviewer for IEEE TITS, EAAI, Pattern Recognition, etc.

Research Interests

Towards Next-Generation Robust Connected Autonomous Driving

My research focuses on developing robust connected autonomous driving systems that operate safely in complex environments. This involves addressing security vulnerabilities in collaborative perception and leveraging foundation models to enhance generalization capabilities of connected agents in unknown scenarios.

Security in Multi-Agent Collaborative Perception

  • Vulnerability Discovery and Attack Exploitation: Investigating data fabrication attacks targeting different stages of collaborative perception by exploiting multi-agent viewpoint asymmetries through adversarial perturbations, including adaptive attacks that dynamically adjust strategies.
  • Robust Defense and Protection Mechanisms: Designing detection and defense frameworks against data fabrication attacks in BEV perspectives using Hypothesis-and-Verification Framework and Autoencoder-based approaches.

Foundation Model-Enhanced Connected Agents

  • World Models for Generative Scenario Synthesis: Utilizing world models with embedded generative modules to synthesize diverse driving scenarios, enabling connected agents to improve robustness and generalization when encountering unseen environments and edge cases.
  • Vision-Language Models for Adaptive Reasoning: Leveraging large vision-language models to enable connected vehicles to understand complex driving contexts and make adaptive decisions through natural language reasoning.
